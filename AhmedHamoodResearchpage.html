Introduction



Computer graphics is the visual representation of information
which is output onto your monitor. Computer graphics can be assessed in detail, usage, and rendering. First, the detail is simply the definition of realism or quality of every pixel throughout your screen. Next, every graphics card or setting implemented into your graphics affects the frames and cuts you will experience. How your monitor and actual computer take the graphics and output them depends on your specifications. This generation has a ton of great but basic graphic implementations built into your computer which help you customize your resolution, also stretching and compressing your graphics is automatically done by most software which adjust your output to your monitor making your graphics more clear and concise.

Raster vs Vector

There are two types of graphics, raster and vector graphics.
Raster graphics or so called bitmap graphics use micro rectangles to build an image. There are lines and non-line graphics which differentiate a simple picture from a more complex picture. Vector graphics use mathematical formulas to develop lines which interconnect to eventually build an image. But, vector graphics also use any other
mathematical ways to develop pictures like dots on a cardinal plane or curves using trigonometry to build pictures.

Imaging is the process of capturing, storing, and displaying images. Graphical imaging uses the process of imaging but includes special effects like rotating to enhance the look and increasing the quality of objects thus giving the graphics a higher usage rate. How Computer Graphics Work

Computer graphics work by showing millions of pixels which are produced with either raster or vector graphics as previously informed about. The CPU works with other software to bring your graphics to life by sending information to the graphics card. The better the graphics card the better your results. Then, your conjunction of information is displayed on a monitor.


To be detailed, a GPU has a processor and RAM which is printed out on a circuit board like a motherboard. But the graphics card is specifically designed for graphics processing and rendering. A recommended specification for increasing a graphics card rendering is a higher PCI, as dual monitors are a popular choice in graphic design. So a higher PCI or motherboard in general can increase productivity and decrease the rendering time.
